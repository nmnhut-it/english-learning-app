<grammar>
## Diễn Đạt Phong Tục Và Truyền Thống

**Nói về phong tục của mình:**
- "In my culture/country, we usually **...**"
  *(Trong văn hóa/đất nước tôi, chúng tôi thường ...)*
- "It's customary to **...**"
  *(Theo phong tục, người ta ...)*
- "We have a tradition of **...**"
  *(Chúng tôi có truyền thống ...)*
- "Every year, we **...**"
  *(Mỗi năm, chúng tôi ...)*

**Phản hồi khi nghe về phong tục người khác:**
- "That's really interesting!"
  *(Thú vị thật!)*
- "We do something similar in ..."
  *(Ở ... chúng tôi cũng làm điều tương tự.)*
- "In our country, we ..."
  *(Ở đất nước chúng tôi, chúng tôi ...)*
- "That's quite different from ..."
  *(Điều đó khá khác với ...)*
</grammar>

<reading>
**Regional Customs and Festivals in Vietnam**
*(Các lễ hội và phong tục vùng miền ở Việt Nam)*

**a. Gióng Festival (Hanoi)**
The Gióng Festival is held at Phù Đổng Temple in Gia Lâm district, Hanoi. It celebrates the legendary warrior Thánh Gióng, who defeated foreign invaders to protect the nation. The festival includes a symbolic battle re-enactment and traditional performances. It was recognised by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2010.

**b. Kate Festival (Ninh Thuận — Cham People)**
The Kate Festival is the most important celebration of the Cham people in Ninh Thuận province. It is a thanksgiving ceremony for the harvest season, honouring Cham gods and ancestors. Participants wear elaborate traditional costumes and perform ritual dances. The festival takes place at ancient Cham towers in late September or early October.

**c. Elephant Racing Festival (Đắk Lắk)**
The Elephant Racing Festival is a traditional celebration of the Tây Nguyên (Central Highlands) ethnic groups. Elephants race along a riverbank while their riders, called "mahouts", encourage them. The festival also includes elephant swimming contests and cultural performances. It highlights the important role of elephants in Tây Nguyên culture and heritage.
</reading>
