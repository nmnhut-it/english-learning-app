**Introduction**

This report describes the problem of single-use plastic waste and suggests two solutions to reduce its negative impact on the environment.

**Problems**

Many single-use plastic products, such as bags, bottles, and straws, are discarded in rubbish bins daily. These plastics take hundreds of years to decompose in landfills, contributing to soil and water pollution. Moreover, burning plastic waste releases toxic gases that harm human health and the environment. Without proper action, plastic pollution will continue to damage ecosystems and wildlife.

**Solutions**

To solve this problem, we suggest two solutions. First, people should be encouraged to use reusable products such as cloth bags, metal straws, and refillable bottles. Governments and businesses could offer incentives, such as discounts for customers who bring their own containers. Second, stricter regulations should be implemented to limit single-use plastics. Supermarkets and restaurants could be required to replace plastic packaging with biodegradable alternatives.

**Conclusion**

Using reusable products and enforcing stricter regulations will significantly reduce plastic waste. We strongly recommend that these solutions be put into practice as soon as possible to protect the environment for future generations.

**Introduction**

This report describes the problem of wasting electricity and water in restrooms due to people leaving the lights on and the water running, and suggests two solutions to address this issue.

**Problems**

Many people leave the lights on and faucets running in restrooms, either by forgetfulness or carelessness. This leads to unnecessary wastage of both electricity and water. Over time, this behavior increases utility costs and contributes to environmental damage, especially in areas facing water shortages and energy conservation challenges.

**Solutions**

To solve this problem, we suggest two solutions. First, automatic sensors could be installed in restrooms to turn off the lights and water when not in use. This would reduce the chances of wastage by automatically controlling energy and water consumption.

**Conclusion**

The implementation of automatic sensors and public education on resource conservation will help minimize waste and contribute to environmental sustainability. Therefore, we recommend that these solutions be put into practice as soon as possible.

Thứ hai, nên triển khai các chiến dịch nâng cao nhận thức để giáo dục mọi người về tầm quan trọng của việc tắt đèn và nước khi rời khỏi nhà vệ sinh. This report describes the problem of electricity and water wastage in the restrooms and suggests two solutions to the problem. 

Leaving lights on in empty restrooms leads to high electricity consumption. As a result, this can significantly increase our energy costs and carbon footprint. Similarly, leaving water running wastes a precious resource, which has a harmful effect on our water supply. These practices reflect a lack of awareness and responsibility towards resource conservation.

To solve this problem, we propose the following solutions. First, we suggest that the school should install automatic lights in all restrooms. Automatic lights will ensure that the lights are only on when the restroom is in use, thereby saving electricity. Second, it is recommended that sensor taps should be installed, which prevents water from being wasted. These solutions are cost-effective in the long run and promote sustainability.

Implementing automatic lights and sensor taps in restrooms will lead to a reduction in our electricity and water wastage. Therefore, I recommend our school put the suggested solutions into practice as soon as possible.
